Dengue fever is a viral illness spread by Aedes aegypti mosquitoes — identified by black and white stripes on their body — that bite during the day unlike malaria mosquitoes which bite at night.
Pakistan experiences severe dengue outbreaks every year especially in Punjab, KPK, and Sindh from July to November after monsoon rains create mosquito breeding conditions.
There is no specific antiviral treatment for dengue — management focuses on supportive care, hydration, and monitoring platelet counts.

Dengue symptoms appear 4-10 days after mosquito bite and include: sudden high fever 103-105F, severe headache behind the eyes, intense muscle and joint pain so severe it is called breakbone fever, skin rash appearing 3-5 days after fever starts, nausea and vomiting, fatigue and weakness.
In classic dengue fever improves within 7-10 days. However warning signs of severe dengue include bleeding from gums or nose, blood in urine or stools, severe abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, rapid breathing, and sudden drop in fever with deterioration.
Dengue shock syndrome and dengue hemorrhagic fever are life-threatening emergencies with dangerous drops in platelet count and internal bleeding.

Platelet count monitoring is critical in dengue: normal platelets are 150,000-400,000. Below 100,000 requires monitoring. Below 50,000 requires hospital admission. Below 20,000 is a medical emergency with high bleeding risk.

Home care for dengue: complete bed rest for the entire duration of fever — do not push yourself to work or move unnecessarily.
Hydration is the most important treatment — drink minimum 3-4 liters of fluids daily including ORS solution, fresh coconut water, fresh papaya leaf juice, pomegranate juice, and plain boiled water.
Papaya leaf juice is the most well-known traditional remedy for dengue — grind fresh papaya leaves, extract juice, drink 30ml twice daily — multiple studies from Pakistan and Asia show it helps increase platelet count.
Fresh pomegranate juice and kiwi juice are rich in Vitamin C and antioxidants supporting immune recovery.
Giloy (guruchi) kadha boiled and consumed helps boost immunity and is traditionally used during dengue recovery.

Never take aspirin, ibuprofen, or diclofenac during dengue — these thin the blood and dramatically increase bleeding risk.
Paracetamol (Panadol) is the only safe fever reducer for dengue — do not exceed 4 tablets in 24 hours.

Prevention: Aedes mosquitoes breed in clean standing water — empty and scrub all water containers weekly including flower pots, water coolers, roof tanks, and buckets.
Use mosquito repellent with DEET on skin and wear full sleeve clothing during daylight hours.
Install window and door screens. Use mosquito nets during daytime naps.
Fumigate home if dengue case is confirmed in the neighborhood.

Seek immediate hospital care if: platelet count drops below 50,000, bleeding from any site, severe abdominal pain, persistent vomiting unable to keep fluids, sudden drop in fever with restlessness or confusion, cold clammy skin with rapid heart rate — these indicate dengue shock.
