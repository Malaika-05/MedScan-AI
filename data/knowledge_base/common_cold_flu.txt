Common cold is caused by over 200 different viruses, most commonly rhinovirus — it is the most frequent illness affecting humans and spreads through respiratory droplets and contaminated surfaces.
Influenza (flu) is caused by influenza viruses A and B — more serious than the common cold with higher fever, severe body aches, and potential for complications.
Both illnesses are viral — antibiotics have absolutely no effect on colds or flu and taking antibiotics unnecessarily creates dangerous antibiotic resistance.

Cold symptoms develop gradually: runny nose progressing from clear to thick yellow-green mucus, nasal congestion and stuffiness, sore throat, sneezing, mild cough, mild fatigue, low-grade fever or no fever, symptoms peak at day 2-3 and resolve in 7-10 days.
Flu symptoms appear suddenly and are more severe: high fever 101-104F, severe muscle aches and body pain, severe headache, extreme fatigue that is disabling, dry cough, chills, and less commonly nausea and vomiting.

Home remedies that genuinely work for cold and flu recovery.
Honey and ginger tea: boil fresh ginger slices in water for 10 minutes, add one teaspoon honey when cooled slightly — honey has antimicrobial properties and coats the throat, ginger is anti-inflammatory. Drink 3-4 cups daily.
Turmeric milk (haldi doodh): one teaspoon turmeric powder in warm milk at bedtime — curcumin in turmeric has powerful anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties proven in research.
Garlic: raw garlic cloves contain allicin which has antiviral properties — eat 2-3 raw cloves daily or add heavily to cooking.
Steam inhalation: inhale steam from a bowl of hot water with 5 drops of eucalyptus oil or menthol for 10-15 minutes twice daily — relieves congestion and soothes irritated airways.
Salt water gargle: half teaspoon salt in a glass of warm water, gargle for 30 seconds several times daily — reduces throat inflammation and washes away viral particles.
Chicken soup or daal shorba (lentil broth): warm liquids relieve congestion, the broth provides electrolytes and nutrients, and steam moistens airways. This is genuinely medically supported.
Naswar or nasal saline rinse: prepare saline with quarter teaspoon salt in 250ml boiled cooled water, rinse each nostril — clears mucus and removes viral particles.
Rest and sleep: the immune system fights infection most effectively during sleep — at least 9-10 hours nightly.
Hydration: drink warm fluids constantly — warm water, herbal teas, and clear broths. Avoid cold water and cold drinks as they worsen throat irritation.

Vitamin C foods: citrus fruits, amla (Indian gooseberry), guava — highest vitamin C content of any fruit available in Pakistan — green chillies all support immune function.
Zinc lozenges or zinc-rich foods like pumpkin seeds, chickpeas, and lentils support faster recovery.

When cold and flu require a doctor visit: fever above 103F lasting more than 3 days, difficulty breathing or shortness of breath, chest pain, confusion or difficulty staying awake, severe vomiting preventing fluid intake, symptoms improving then suddenly worsening — this pattern suggests bacterial superinfection requiring antibiotics.
Children under 2, adults over 65, pregnant women, and people with diabetes or heart disease should see a doctor early as they are at high risk for complications.
Flu can lead to pneumonia especially in vulnerable populations — this is life-threatening and requires hospital treatment.
