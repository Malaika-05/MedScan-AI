Gastroenteritis is inflammation of the stomach and intestines usually caused by viral or bacterial infection from contaminated food or water — extremely common in Pakistan due to food safety and water quality issues.
Peptic ulcer disease occurs when the protective lining of the stomach or small intestine erodes — caused by H. pylori bacteria (very common in Pakistan affecting 70-80% of adults) or overuse of pain medications like ibuprofen and aspirin.
Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) occurs when stomach acid regularly flows back up into the esophagus causing heartburn — worsened by spicy foods, fatty foods, tea, and lying flat after eating.

Gastroenteritis symptoms: watery diarrhea multiple times daily, nausea and vomiting, abdominal cramps and pain, fever, and dehydration.
Dehydration is the main danger of gastroenteritis especially in children and elderly — signs include dry mouth, dark urine or no urine, sunken eyes, extreme weakness, and dizziness on standing.

Home treatment for gastroenteritis and diarrhea.
ORS (Oral Rehydration Salts) is the most important treatment — available at all pharmacies for very low cost or make at home: 6 level teaspoons sugar plus half teaspoon salt in 1 liter clean boiled water. Drink continuously throughout the day.
For adults — 200-400ml ORS after each loose stool. For children — 50-100ml after each stool.
BRAT diet during recovery: Bananas (B), boiled Rice (R), Apple (A) or Apple sauce without sugar, dry Toast (T) or plain khushk roti — these foods are easy to digest and help firm stools.
Dahi (yogurt) with live cultures is highly beneficial — probiotics in yogurt restore gut bacteria and reduce diarrhea duration.
Rice kanji or chawal ka paani: boil rice in excess water, strain and drink the starchy water with a pinch of salt — traditional Pakistani remedy proven to reduce diarrhea.
Guava leaves tea: boil 5-6 guava leaves in 1.5 cups water for 10 minutes, strain and drink — contains tannins that have antidiarrheal properties.
Ajwain (carom seeds) with salt: half teaspoon ajwain with a pinch of salt and warm water relieves gas, bloating, and abdominal cramps quickly.
Avoid dairy (except dahi), oily food, spicy food, raw vegetables, and fruits with high fiber during acute gastroenteritis.

Peptic ulcer home management: eat small frequent meals every 2-3 hours — empty stomach increases acid damage to ulcers.
Avoid tea on empty stomach — this is the single most damaging habit for stomach lining in Pakistan where morning tea on empty stomach is culturally common.
Avoid NSAIDs (ibuprofen, diclofenac, aspirin) completely — these directly damage stomach lining and worsen ulcers.
Cabbage juice: half cup fresh cabbage juice daily is a traditional remedy with some evidence for healing stomach ulcers due to glutamine content.
Banana: raw bananas coat and protect stomach lining — eat 1-2 daily. Also neutralize excess acid.
Cold milk without sugar provides temporary relief from burning ulcer pain by neutralizing acid — however excessive milk consumption can worsen ulcers long-term.
H. pylori is treated with a triple antibiotic regimen prescribed by a doctor — if you have diagnosed H. pylori, complete the full treatment course.

Seek immediate emergency care for: blood in stool (bright red or black tarry stools indicate internal bleeding), severe abdominal pain that is constant and worsening, vomiting blood, signs of severe dehydration especially in children (no urine for 8 hours, sunken eyes, very dry mouth), high fever with abdominal pain suggesting appendicitis.
