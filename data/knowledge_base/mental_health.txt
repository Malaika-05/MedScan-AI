Mental health conditions are medical illnesses just like diabetes or hypertension — they are caused by chemical imbalances in the brain, genetic factors, and life experiences. They are not weakness, laziness, or lack of faith.
Depression, anxiety, and stress-related disorders are extremely common in Pakistan but severely underdiagnosed and undertreated due to stigma, lack of awareness, and insufficient mental health services.
Approximately 34% of Pakistanis suffer from anxiety and depression — one of the highest rates globally — worsened by poverty, instability, displacement, and domestic stress.

Depression symptoms: persistent sadness or emptiness lasting more than 2 weeks, loss of interest in activities previously enjoyed, changes in appetite and weight, sleeping too much or inability to sleep, fatigue and loss of energy, difficulty concentrating, feelings of worthlessness or excessive guilt, slowed thinking and movement, withdrawal from family and social life.
Severe depression can cause thoughts of death or suicide — this is a medical emergency requiring immediate psychiatric care.

Anxiety symptoms: excessive worry that is difficult to control, restlessness and feeling on edge, muscle tension especially in neck and shoulders, sleep difficulties, irritability, rapid heartbeat and shortness of breath during anxiety attacks, avoidance of feared situations.

Home strategies that genuinely help mental wellbeing.
Physical activity: 30 minutes of brisk walking daily has proven antidepressant effects comparable to some medications — activates endorphins and serotonin production.
Regular prayer and spiritual connection: namaz performed 5 times daily with genuine mindfulness serves as structured breathing exercise, mindfulness practice, and provides community connection — documented to reduce anxiety and depression in Muslim populations.
Social connection: isolation worsens depression — stay connected with family and trusted friends even when you do not want to.
Sleep hygiene: consistent sleep and wake times, no phone screen 1 hour before sleep, no heavy meal within 2 hours of sleep — poor sleep severely worsens anxiety and depression.
Journaling: writing feelings in a notebook without judgment helps process difficult emotions and identify thought patterns.
Limit news consumption — constant negative news exposure increases anxiety. Allocate specific limited times for news.

Foods that support mental health: omega-3 rich foods including fish, walnuts, and flaxseeds support brain function. Magnesium-rich foods including dark leafy greens, nuts, seeds, and dark chocolate reduce anxiety. Probiotic foods like dahi support the gut-brain axis.
Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) root powder — available at herbal shops in Pakistan — half teaspoon in warm milk at bedtime has evidence for reducing cortisol (stress hormone) and anxiety.

If someone expresses thoughts of suicide or self-harm: take it seriously, stay with them, listen without judgment, remove means of self-harm from the environment, and get them to a psychiatric facility or doctor immediately.

Seek professional help when: depression or anxiety symptoms last more than 2 weeks, symptoms significantly interfere with daily life or work, person has thoughts of suicide, person is using alcohol or substances to cope, or physical symptoms with no medical cause persist (pain, fatigue, dizziness with normal tests — these can be anxiety manifestations).
