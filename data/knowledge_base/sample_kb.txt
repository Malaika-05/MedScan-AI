Diabetes mellitus is a chronic condition where blood sugar levels are too high.
Type 2 diabetes symptoms include frequent urination, excessive thirst, fatigue, and blurred vision.
Normal fasting blood glucose: 70-100 mg/dL. Pre-diabetes: 100-125 mg/dL. Diabetes: above 126 mg/dL.
Management includes diet control, regular exercise, and medication as prescribed by a doctor.
Patients should monitor blood glucose regularly and avoid high-sugar foods.

Malaria is caused by Plasmodium parasites transmitted through Anopheles mosquito bites.
Symptoms include high fever with chills, sweating, headache, and muscle pain.
Fever in malaria is cyclical — it spikes every 48-72 hours depending on the parasite type.
Malaria is common in rural Pakistan especially during and after monsoon season.
Seek immediate medical care if fever exceeds 103F with chills and shaking.

Tuberculosis (TB) is a bacterial infection primarily affecting the lungs.
Key symptoms: persistent cough over 3 weeks, coughing blood, night sweats, unexplained weight loss.
TB is treatable with a 6-month antibiotic course — never stop treatment early.
Pakistan has high TB burden — free treatment is available at government DOTS centers.

Typhoid fever is caused by Salmonella typhi bacteria through contaminated food and water.
Symptoms: sustained high fever, weakness, stomach pain, headache, loss of appetite.
Common in areas with poor sanitation — boil drinking water and wash hands regularly.
Widal test and blood culture are used for diagnosis.

Hypertension (high blood pressure) often has no symptoms — called the silent killer.
Normal blood pressure: below 120/80 mmHg. Hypertension: 140/90 mmHg or above.
Risk factors include excess salt intake, obesity, stress, smoking, and family history.
Lifestyle changes: reduce salt, exercise 30 min daily, avoid smoking, manage stress.
