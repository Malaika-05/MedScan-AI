Fungal skin infections are extremely common in Pakistan due to hot humid climate, water scarcity limiting hygiene, and shared clothing or towels.
Common fungal infections include ringworm (circular itchy patches), tinea versicolor (white or brown patches on back and chest especially after sun exposure), athlete's foot (between toes), and candidal infections in skin folds.
Fungal infections are NOT caused by poor character or lifestyle — they are opportunistic infections worsened by heat and moisture.

Home remedies for fungal skin infections.
Coconut oil: apply pure coconut oil to affected area twice daily — contains caprylic acid and lauric acid with proven antifungal properties.
Neem leaves paste: grind neem leaves into paste and apply to affected skin — neem has powerful natural antifungal and antibacterial properties.
Aloe vera gel: apply fresh aloe vera gel from the plant directly to itchy infected areas — soothes inflammation and has antifungal properties.
Apple cider vinegar diluted 1:1 with water applied with cotton to affected area — creates acidic environment inhibiting fungal growth. Do not use on broken or cracked skin as it stings.
Garlic paste: crush 2-3 garlic cloves, apply paste to affected area for 20 minutes — allicin in garlic has strong antifungal activity.
Tea tree oil diluted in coconut oil (5 drops tea tree oil in one tablespoon coconut oil) — powerful antifungal, do not apply undiluted.
Keep affected areas clean and completely dry — fungus thrives in moisture. Apply powder (talcum or antifungal powder) in skin folds.
Wear loose cotton clothing — synthetic fabrics trap heat and moisture worsening fungal infections.
Wash clothes in hot water and dry in direct sunlight which kills fungal spores.
Do not share towels, clothing, or shoes with others.

Over-the-counter antifungal creams like clotrimazole (Canesten) are effective for most fungal infections — apply twice daily for minimum 2 weeks even after the rash disappears to prevent recurrence.

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune skin condition causing thick red scaly patches — not contagious, not caused by diet, and has no cure but can be well-managed.
Psoriasis flares are triggered by stress, skin injuries, infections, certain medications, and cold dry weather.
Moisturize affected skin multiple times daily with petroleum jelly (Vaseline) or thick moisturizing creams — prevents cracking and reduces scaling.
Aloe vera gel applied directly provides cooling and mild anti-inflammatory relief for psoriasis plaques.
Dead Sea salt baths or epsom salt soaks reduce inflammation and scaling — dissolve 2 cups salt in warm bath, soak 15 minutes.
Avoid scratching psoriasis plaques — this causes Koebner phenomenon where new lesions develop at scratch sites.

Acne management: wash face twice daily with mild soap and lukewarm water — not hot water which strips natural oils causing rebound oiliness.
Multani mitti (Fuller's earth) face pack once weekly absorbs excess oil and reduces blackheads.
Neem face wash or neem soap reduces acne-causing bacteria.
Rose water as toner after washing tightens pores and balances skin pH.
Honey and cinnamon mask applied for 20 minutes then rinsed has antibacterial properties.
Avoid squeezing or popping pimples — this pushes bacteria deeper, worsens inflammation, and causes permanent scarring.

See a dermatologist for: rash spreading rapidly, skin infection with pus, fever with skin rash, rash not improving after 2 weeks of home treatment, or any suspicious mole or skin growth changing in size or color.
