Tuberculosis (TB) is a serious bacterial infection caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis that primarily attacks the lungs but can spread to brain, spine, kidneys, and lymph nodes.
Pakistan has one of the highest TB burdens in the world with approximately 600,000 new cases annually — it is a major public health crisis.
TB spreads through the air when an infected person coughs, sneezes, speaks, or spits — tiny droplets containing bacteria remain suspended in air for hours.

Primary TB symptoms: persistent cough lasting more than 2-3 weeks is the most important warning sign.
Other symptoms include coughing up blood or blood-stained mucus, chest pain when breathing or coughing, unexplained weight loss of more than 5kg in a month, severe fatigue and weakness, fever especially in afternoons and evenings, drenching night sweats that soak clothing and bedding.
Extrapulmonary TB symptoms depend on the organ affected — neck swelling in lymph node TB, back pain in spinal TB, headache and stiff neck in brain TB.

TB diagnosis: sputum test (examining cough mucus under microscope) is the first test. GeneXpert test gives accurate results in 2 hours and detects drug-resistant TB. Chest X-ray shows lung damage patterns. Tuberculin skin test (TST/Mantoux) shows past exposure.

Treatment: TB is completely curable with a full course of antibiotics — typically 6 months for regular TB, 9-24 months for drug-resistant TB.
The most critical rule: NEVER stop TB medication even when you feel better after 2-3 months — stopping early creates drug-resistant TB that is much harder to treat and more dangerous.
Standard treatment uses 4 drugs simultaneously: Isoniazid, Rifampicin, Pyrazinamide, and Ethambutol for the first 2 months, then 2 drugs for the remaining 4 months.
DOTS (Directly Observed Treatment Short-course) program: a health worker watches you take medication daily to ensure completion — available free at all government health centers in Pakistan.

Free TB treatment: complete TB treatment including all medicines is available FREE at all government hospitals, TB centers, and Basic Health Units in Pakistan under the National TB Control Program.
Patients should never pay for TB medicine — if asked to pay, report it to the district health office.

Home care during TB treatment: eat nutritious high-protein diet — eggs, lentils (daal), meat, milk, and cheese help the body fight infection and recover from weight loss.
Increase vitamin D through sun exposure and foods — TB patients are often deficient in vitamin D which impairs immune response.
Giloy (Tinospora cordifolia) kadha is a traditional remedy used to boost immunity during TB recovery — boil stems in water and drink.
Garlic has natural antimicrobial properties — include 2-3 raw garlic cloves daily in diet as a supportive measure.
Turmeric milk (haldi doodh) — one teaspoon turmeric in warm milk daily — has anti-inflammatory properties helpful during recovery.
Rest adequately, avoid smoking and alcohol completely, sleep in a well-ventilated room with windows open.
Isolate for at least 2 weeks after starting treatment — wear a surgical mask when around others, especially children.

If TB is untreated the infection destroys lung tissue causing permanent breathing difficulty, spreads to other organs, and is fatal in 50% of untreated cases within 5 years.
Drug-resistant TB (MDR-TB) requires 18-24 months of treatment with more toxic drugs and has much lower cure rates.
Seek immediate care if: coughing blood, difficulty breathing, high fever with chest pain, confusion, or severe weight loss.
