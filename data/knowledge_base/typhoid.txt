Typhoid fever is a serious bacterial infection caused by Salmonella typhi bacteria, spread through contaminated food and water — a major problem in Pakistan due to poor water quality and sanitation.
Pakistan is currently experiencing extensively drug-resistant typhoid (XDR typhoid) outbreaks, making it critical to complete full treatment and not self-medicate.

Typhoid symptoms develop gradually over 1-3 weeks after infection.
Week 1: slowly rising fever that increases each day reaching 103-104F, headache, general weakness, loss of appetite, dry cough.
Week 2: sustained high fever, severe abdominal pain, distended abdomen, constipation followed by diarrhea, rose-colored spots may appear on chest.
Week 3: if untreated — intestinal bleeding or perforation (hole in intestine) which is a surgical emergency and life-threatening.

Diagnosis: Widal test is the most common test in Pakistan but has low accuracy — blood culture is the gold standard and takes 3-5 days. Typhidot (typhoid IgM test) is faster and more reliable.
Never rely only on Widal test results — confirm with a doctor who considers full clinical picture.

Treatment: XDR typhoid in Pakistan is resistant to most older antibiotics — Azithromycin is now the first-line treatment. Ceftriaxone injection for severe cases. Full course of 7-14 days is essential — never stop when fever decreases.

Home care during typhoid: diet is extremely important — eat soft, easily digestible foods only.
Safe foods: khichdi (rice and lentil porridge), boiled rice, mashed potato, banana, boiled egg whites, fresh yogurt, coconut water, ORS solution for hydration.
Strictly avoid: raw vegetables, spicy food, oily fried food, street food, unboiled water, raw salads, whole grains, and meat with bones — these can trigger intestinal complications.
Stay extremely well hydrated — typhoid causes high fever and sweating leading to dangerous dehydration. Drink 3-4 liters of boiled cooled water daily.
Saunf (fennel seeds) boiled in water as a tea helps soothe abdominal pain and aids digestion during typhoid recovery.
Basil and ginger tea with honey helps reduce fever and provides comfort — boil 10 basil leaves with small piece of ginger, add honey when cooled.
Complete bed rest is essential — do not return to work or school until fever-free for at least 48 hours.

Prevention: only drink boiled or filtered water — even in cities pipe water is not safe in Pakistan.
Wash hands thoroughly with soap before eating and after using toilet — typhoid spreads through fecal-oral route.
Eat freshly cooked hot food only — avoid food that has been sitting out for hours or stored at room temperature.
Typhoid vaccine (Typhim Vi) is available and recommended especially for children in Pakistan — provides 3 years of protection.
Vi-conjugate typhoid vaccine is now part of Pakistan's Expanded Programme on Immunization (EPI) for children.

Emergency signs requiring immediate hospital admission: sudden severe abdominal pain (intestinal perforation), black or bloody stools (internal bleeding), confusion or altered consciousness, persistent vomiting unable to keep fluids down, fever above 104F not responding to medication, abdominal rigidity.
Intestinal perforation from untreated typhoid is a surgical emergency with high mortality — do not delay hospital care.
